How does TENS work?

TENS, or Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation, works by delivering low-voltage electrical currents through electrode pads placed on the skin. These gentle pulses block or interfere with the transmission of pain signals, reducing the sensation of pain experienced.

TENS technology was developed in the late 1960s, and was initially used for pain management and has since been adapted and refined for various therapeutic purposes, including managing chronic and acute pain.

How it feels?

The sensation experienced with a TENS device can vary among individuals. Generally, when using a TENS unit, people may feel a tingling, buzzing, or pulsating sensation at or around the area where the electrodes are placed on the skin. Some describe it as a gentle, soothing sensation, while others might perceive it as a mild, rhythmic tapping or fluttering feeling. It's essential to start with a lower intensity level and adjust it gradually to a comfortable level for effective pain relief without causing discomfort.
